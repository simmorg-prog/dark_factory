## What This Framework Is

A structured body of work describing how engineering organisations evolve their use of AI — from individual prompt use through to fully autonomous, agent-operated software delivery. It is organised into six stages, each cumulative: you cannot skip stages, and each one builds directly on the disciplines below it.

The framework is written for three primary audiences: **engineers**, **architects**, and **technology leaders (CTOs/VPs)**. Each audience has a different entry point and a different set of sections that will be most immediately useful.

## Reading Paths by Audience

### 👩‍💻 Engineers
*You build and ship software. You want to understand what changes about your day-to-day work as AI capability increases.*

| Step | Read | Why |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | [Glossary of Terms](./glossary.md) | Get the vocabulary right — especially the six disciplines and the three actor types |
| 2 | [Maturity Curve Overview](./maturity-curve.md) | See the full arc at a glance, including where your organisation sits today |
| 3 | Stage 1–3 Deep Dives (Wave 4) | Understand what good practice looks like at the stages you are currently working in |
| 4 | [The Human Role Transformation](../01-actors/human-role.md) (Wave 2) | Understand where your role is heading |
| 5 | Stage Transition Playbooks (Wave 4) | The practical guide for moving from one stage to the next |

**Skip for now:** Organisational maturity assessment, investment planning, governance — those are for your leadership team.

## A Note on Completeness

This document is built breadth-first. Every section exists at overview depth before any section is deepened. If you reach a cross-reference that leads to a stub or a short overview, that is expected — the section exists but has not yet been deepened. Check the [BACKLOG](../BACKLOG.md) for current status.
